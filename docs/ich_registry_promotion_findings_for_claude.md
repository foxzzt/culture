# ICH Registry Promotion Findings for Claude

Date checked: 2026-07-06

## Bottom line

The evidence supports treating promotion from provincial ICH project status to national ICH project status as **additive, not substitutive**, at least for the project registry variable.

No official rule found in the checked national, Guangdong, or Jilin documents says that a provincial representative ICH project or inheritor must be removed from the provincial list after being selected at the national level. The strongest cross-check is Guangdong's current official project spreadsheet: Yueju/Cantonese opera appears with both provincial and national batch fields.

Recommended manuscript action:

- For project-count/IPR variables, delete the caveat that national promotion may cause provincial-list exit and distort IPR.
- For inheritor-count variables only, keep a narrow note if needed: some management/assessment practices separate "national-level inheritors" from "provincial-level inheritors" in annual evaluation statistics, so inheritor statistics may need careful level definitions.

## National Rule Checked

### National representative inheritor rules

Document: `国家级非物质文化遗产代表性传承人认定与管理办法`

Authority / number: Ministry of Culture and Tourism Order No. 3 (`中华人民共和国文化和旅游部令第3号`)

Status/date: adopted 2019-11-12, issued 2019-11-29, effective 2020-03-01

Official URL: https://zwgk.mct.gov.cn/zfxxgkml/zcfg/bmgz/202012/t20201214_919516.html

Relevant finding:

- Searched for `晋升`, `升格`, `调整`, `退出`, and cancellation-related terms.
- No clause says a local/provincial inheritor loses local/provincial status after national recognition.
- Article 22 lists cancellation of national inheritor qualification: loss of PRC nationality, fraud, two failed evaluations without proper reason, serious adverse legal/social conduct, voluntary waiver, and other cancellation circumstances.
- Article 24 says provinces may formulate local inheritor recognition and management rules by reference to this national rule.

## Provincial Rules Checked

### Guangdong representative inheritor rules

Document: `广东省省级非物质文化遗产代表性传承人认定与管理办法`

Authority / number: `粤文旅规〔2021〕2号`

Status/date: current/effective, issued on Guangdong Culture and Tourism Department site, 2022-01-04

Official URL: https://whly.gd.gov.cn/gkmlpt/content/3/3737/post_3737114.html

Relevant finding:

- Searched for `晋升`, `升格`, `国家级`, `退出`, `取消`, and related terms.
- No clause says promotion to national-level inheritor status cancels provincial-level inheritor status.
- Article 22 cancellation grounds are loss of PRC nationality, fraud, two failed evaluations without proper reason, serious adverse legal/social conduct, voluntary waiver, and other cancellation circumstances.
- Article 23 covers loss of transmission capacity and possible listing as an honorary provincial inheritor; this is not promotion-related.

## Suggested Wording for the Paper

Use this for project/IPR measurement:

> Official rules and cross-checks do not support treating national recognition as removing a project from provincial registries. In Guangdong's current official list of provincial-and-above ICH representative projects, Yueju/Cantonese opera is recorded with both provincial and national batch identifiers. We therefore treat provincial and national recognitions as cumulative registry layers rather than mutually exclusive statuses.

If an inheritor-count caveat is needed:

> Inheritor management statistics may use a narrower "provincial-level excluding national-level inheritors" evaluation scope in some administrative reports. This affects interpretation of inheritor-level counts, but it does not imply that national project recognition removes a project from provincial project registries.
